Dear Editor,

Please consider the manuscript "Study Overlap Across 501 Cochrane Systematic Reviews: Quantifying Non-Independence for Umbrella Reviews and Methodological Research" for publication in Research Synthesis Methods.

Umbrella reviews and meta-research often assume that systematic reviews contribute independent evidence, yet the extent of primary-study overlap across large review collections is rarely quantified empirically. We mapped all primary studies across 501 Cochrane reviews in the Pairwise70 dataset, identified 10,006 unique studies, and computed the Corrected Covered Area and pairwise Jaccard indices across all 125,250 review pairs. Overlap was minimal overall, but 604 review pairs still shared at least one study, showing where non-independence remains relevant.

The manuscript fits the journal because it provides a reproducible methodological assessment directly relevant to evidence-synthesis practice. The results give practical guidance for umbrella review authors and for researchers who use Pairwise70 as a benchmarking dataset.

The manuscript has not been published or submitted elsewhere. The author has approved the submission and agrees to be accountable for the work. Analysis code and output data are available in the linked repository.
