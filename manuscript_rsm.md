## Abstract

**Background:** When multiple systematic reviews include the same primary studies, synthesising these reviews (umbrella reviews) creates non-independence that can bias estimates and inflate confidence.

**Methods:** We mapped all 10,006 unique primary studies across 501 Cochrane systematic reviews from the Pairwise70 dataset, computing the Corrected Covered Area (CCA) index and pairwise Jaccard overlap between all 125,250 review pairs.

**Results:** 444 studies (4.4%) appeared in more than one review, with a maximum of 7 reviews per study. The CCA was 0.0001 (classified as "Slight" overlap). Of 125,250 possible review pairs, 604 (0.5%) shared at least one study, with the most overlapping pair sharing 43 studies (Jaccard = 0.15). The low overlap validates the Pairwise70 dataset as a source of largely independent meta-analyses for methodological research.

**Conclusions:** Study overlap in Cochrane reviews is minimal (CCA = 0.0001), supporting the use of this dataset for large-scale methodological studies. However, 604 review pairs do share studies, and umbrella reviews drawing from overlapping Cochrane reviews should quantify and account for this non-independence.
